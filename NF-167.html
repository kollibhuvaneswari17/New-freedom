Continuous Support Across the 167-Hour Gap.

The Care Gap

The Structural Gap
in Modern Care

In the standard model of care, a patient may receive clinical supervision for just one hour a week. That leaves 167 hours when the individual is largely on their own.

  • The Standard: One hour of therapy. One hundred sixty-seven hours without continuous support.
  • The Risk Zone: Relapse often happens in the gap, far from the clinic and far from immediate help.
  • The System Failure: Paper journals and static apps go quiet when the patient enters the gap.

Conclusion: the care model needs an operating layer that remains present between clinical encounters.

Continuous Care Architecture

A Clinical Operating Layer, Not a Consumer Wellness App

NF-167 is not designed as a passive consumer wellness tool. It is a clinical operating layer that brings together biometric data, AI context, and peer support within one care environment.

Its purpose is to remain close at hand throughout the day, helping preserve continuity of care regardless of time or location.

Structured Daily Engagement

A Framework for Daily Consistency

In early recovery, cognitive overload can become a major relapse trigger. By simplifying the daily dashboard into three non-negotiable actions, the Daily Trio reduces friction and helps prevent overwhelm.

The daily flow asks users to log their biometrics, complete guided neural regulation, and pursue physical activation. Micro-consistency builds macro-resilience.

Context-Aware Support

Immediate Support in Context

Atlas is not a generic chatbot. It is an intelligent support engine that reads the user's profile, current phasing, past biometric entries, and historical triggers.

When distress spikes, Atlas can deliver immediate, personalised grounding techniques without forcing the user to wait for the next scheduled session.
